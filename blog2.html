Design approach 1: Speculative design

Speculative design

What are the key features of Speculative Design?
- A method of design that allows us to think about the future - to branch out and create questions on the potential
- A different method of design thinking from the traditional, it asks questions rather than answers
- Its based on the imagination - the limit is the designers imagination
- because of this nature of design thinking
- this type of design allows us to think about the future and to head/create the future we would want
- it also allows us to see new tech and now tech from the current would be affecting us in the future
- can also help us in preventing a future we don’t want- a more dystopian future or help us to work towards a future we want - such as a utopia


How does it differ from the design frameworks you’ve encountered on the Design programme so far?
- So far, the only other design framework we have looked into is the double diamond
- Which asks questions, but the final outcome is an answer to the problem
- this design framework begins by ideating, which asks some questions within its framework first, and then it iterations into an answer
- making it so that the final answer of the design brief/question is an answer to the equation
- this is a more critical type of design thinking as it analyses the question and creates an answer

Speculative design
- design thinking which asks questions, and the final outcome is a question
- Its broads out - more divergent thinking and solutions
- They allow us to think more about the future - what ‘worlds to create
- Placing the designer in the centre instead of the client/target person
- It allows us to be creative and be out there
- it also creates worlds - rather than products

Beyond Speculative Design


Some strengths
- It allows for many possibilities
- Allows us to be creative in our thinking
- It is a method that allows us to think about the future and question what we know and what we could possibly do later on to change.
- Creating questions and creating changes with the possibilities of the designs

Limitations
- It's not that practical - the output doesn't lead to much
- It doesn't give any means
- It creates ‘worlds’ rather than products (ideas so it could not be as concrete if they wasn't looked into in a more affirmative manner)
- criticism on how its created with a western mindset - so that the worlds imagined that are in the near future also links or can be obtained from ppl in places of privilege
- They don't think about the other scenarios of others and only of themselves.
- The link with the pluriverse

Future of Jobs - case study

Describe the issues it examined and the methods it used to communicate them. What was the overall goal of the design?
A research project was done by two master's students (Wongsathon Choonhavan and Daphne Gerodimou) Focus on educating the future generation of the job market - goal Jobs were stable but now not
- report from World Economic Forum
- 65% of children in primary school now will be working in jobs that didn't exist now
They communicated this within 4 steps
- Changing perception
- Imagining a future job
- Role play
- Future skills

They did workshops with children to help them start to create their own careers that could be potentially in the future
- out of the box thinking
- Used templates
passion map
- Helped the childrens to reflect on themselves and their skills
One day scenario
- A guide into the future and what they would see
Making a magic machine for your future job
- Creating, prototyping for what they could be working with in the future
Collage toy
- Was made to allow for users to do in their own time, a toy that helps with the children's creativity by creating paper collages of what their futures could be
Instagram accounts
Gachapon
- Created job concepts and collected some from the public - Instagram
- Inspire children about their future jobs - for ones who are scared to think about the future or who hasn't thought about it yet
- It gives a QR code to their Instagram - for more information

Why did you find it a more successful example of speculative design than the other cases your group analysed? How did it better achieve its goal?
It was much more interactive with their methods of communication
Workshops, many templates and activities listed, allowed for more spread and awareness of the topic and helped fulfil the want or goal of getting children thinking about their future jobs - what they would want to do
In the workshops and activities,it didn't create the future or the project didn't make or present a future job and communicated it within that manner to question if children wanted these potential jobs that were given to them - however, they asked more questions and spread the imaginative and questioning nature of speculative design with the target audience
The Instagram account does give potential ideas of future jobs - presenting their ideas of the future, and also the interactivity with social media allows for ideas to be shared on the topic of future jobs for children to be made
This allows for the children to think about how these are possibilities of jobs, to think further than a policemen or a teacher - standard jobs
